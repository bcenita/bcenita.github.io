UWMC Medical Provider Evaluations

Timeline: Apr 2017 - June 2016

Role: UX Advocate

Tools: Sketch, inVision, Google Draw

Skills: Information System Analysis and Design

Description: An online platform serving to automate and replace the current paper-based system of UWMC's Medical Provider Evaluations.

Key Takeaways:

  • Gained knowledge of the System Development Life Cycle.
  • There are actually systems that exist, even within UW, that are still completely paper-based and that's a problem!
  • Although the class was focused on Information System Analysis, I took it upon myself to think and design with an eye towards creating an optimal User Experience for all parties involved.
inVision Prototype

Context

In a course focused on Information System Analysis and Design, we were tasked to create a completely automated process for the UWMC Medical Staff evaluations for our client Madeline (the UWMC Medical Staff Credentialing Specialist). Her goals and needs included:

  • A user-friendly system, front end and back end
  • Ability to manage compliance
  • Track those who have recieved and responded to notifications
  • Provide reports on evaluation statuses

Problem Space: Current State Analysis

There are a number or risks and concerns that are a result of operating through a completely paper-based system:

  • Risk of losing physical document
  • Hard to track responses
  • Madeline has to review every single assessment
  • It is very time consuming
  • Slow, disorganized, and delayed
  • A lack of efficiency and effectiveness
  • Information connected is not secure
  • Paper is very costly

Solution

Thus, our proposed solution is an online platform that interacts with a centralized database to eliminate paper pushing and the back and forth nature of the current process.

Features:

  • Automated notification system for requests, deadlines, etc.
  • Online forms to replace the paper based provider forms
  • Automated reporting system, specialized to show performance trends and engender effective actions
  • Automated tracking and managing of outstanding requests
  • Inherent communication system
  • Effortless user interface
