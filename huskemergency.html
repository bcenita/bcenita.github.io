Context

In a course focused on Design Thinking, we were tasked to identify a type of disaster problem and propose a solution that included digital and physical components. My team and I decided to revamp a technology used at all scales of disasters - the first aid kit.

Problem Space

There were three main problems we identified with the current state of first aid kits:

  1. Most people are unfamiliar with the contents of first aid kits and many first aid kit practices
  2. In general, the contents of a first-aid kit are very unorganized
  3. Specifically on the UW Campus, many of our peers were not sure where they would go in the event of the need for first aid

Solution

"Husk.Emergency" is an enterprise-level first aid kit to be implemented on the UW Campus, targeted towards students and staff in the event of any disaster or emergency. When designing our solution, we wanted to make sure that both the physical and digital components would be easily usable by someone who may be under high-levels of stress during an emergency.

Features:

  • Provides users with all the supplies of a class B first-aid kit required by OSHA in an organized and usable way
  • The physical kit itself contains multiple compartments, systematically organizing supplies to be consistent with the information provided by the interface
  • Each compartment is numbered and grouped by color, allowing users to find items quickly and easily
  • Interface provides two majors categories of information: step by step instructions on how to use the contents in various first-aid procedures and disaster safety information and protocol specific to the UW campus.
